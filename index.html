My journalistic experience

Last semester, I served as a beat reporter covering K-12 and youth issues for the Columbia Missourian. That role quickly evolved into regular coverage of Columbia Public Schools, including school board meetings and education-related developments across the district. I found the work challenging and very meaningful. Because of that experience, I'm continuing to cover Columbia Public Schools this semester through an independent study. The focus is ongoing beat reporting on K-12 and youth issues in Columbia, with consistent coverage of CPS board meetings and related education news. The goal is to deepen my reporting on education policy, equity and student outcomes, while expanding enterprise and contextual coverage that goes beyond day-to-day meeting stories. K-12 education has a direct and lasting impact on the local community, and it deserves sustained, knowledgeable reporting. This independent study allows me to devote focused time and attention to the beat beyond the scope of a traditional classroom setting. I am very excited and grateful to be working under the supervision of Professor Brixey.

Next year, I will be working as an Education Reporter for the Jefferson City News Tribune in Jefferson City, Missouri, covering K12, higher education, and education policy in Jefferson City and Mid-Missouri. I will get to illuminate how education decisions, funding, and policies shape the lives of students, families, educators, and our community. I am excited to dive into coverage of the Jefferson City School District, area K12 districts, Lincoln University of Missouri, State Technical College of Missouri, the University of Missouri Board of Curators, and statewide education agencies. My responsibilities will be to report daily and enterprise stories on education institutions and policy Attend school board meetings and education events as needed. Also, to develop sources and pursue watchdog and accountability reporting and gather. Verify information through interviews, documents, and public records. Write clear, accurate stories in AP style. Collaborate with editors on story development and presentation. Occasionally take photos or record basic video.
